Introduction

The Ojos Project is looking for individuals to join! We are a research project under UC Irvine professor Mark S. Baldwin's Undergraduate Research Lab.

The benefits of joining the project include:

  • A course grade (IN4MATX 199)
  • Research experience
  • Linux/Raspberry Pi experience (and potentially Lua and Rust)

What are we doing?

We're researching the needs of hospice patients and caregivers to provide them with an easier way for caregivers to manage their loved one's care. Our plan is to create a device that will help synchronize important details of their loved one's care, such as medications and other care instructions.

Our current plan is to use tools such as the Raspberry Pi OS to build this system while experimenting with Linux Window Managers such as the awesome window manager. This may also include programming languages such as Lua and Rust.

All of our software will be for the good of those in need, and will be 100% free and open-source.

Member Responsibilities

Considering we're being graded for this project, there will be responsibilities for this project. At the very minimum, the responsibilities include:

  • A minimum of a two-quarter commitment
  • Meeting at least twice a week (once with Prof. Baldwin, another to work)
  • Meeting responsibilities as outlined in our internal timeline

What are we looking for?

We're looking for individuals who already know, or are curious about, various technologies such as Linux, programming languages, or modifying existing software. Our end goal is to create a device that synchronizes hospice patient health to make life easier for caregivers.

If you want to work with a Raspberry Pi, Linux, programming languages, or even just writing documentation for our work, we're looking for you!
